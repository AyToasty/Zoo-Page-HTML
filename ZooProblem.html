Zoo Animals

Information regarding various animals

Bears

  • Ollie

    • The Grizzly Bear which is a subspecies of the brown bears inhabiting North America. Standing as tall as 8 feet and weighing up to 800 pounds. They are highly intelligent and fast, reaching speeds up to 35 miles per hour.

  • Mona

    • The Polar Bear is a close relative of the brown bear but resides in the Artic. One of the largest land based predators. Polar bears can grow up to 8 feet long and weighing up to 1700 pounds. They have large paws to help them navigate through the ice.

Giraffes

  • Frankie

    • The Masai Giraffe, also spelled Massai Giraffe, also known as Kilimanjaro giraffe,is a subspecies of the giraffe . It is native to East Africa. The Massai Giraffe is distinguished by jagged and irregular spots on its body. It is the largest bodied giraffe species making it the tallest land animal on Earth.

  • Coconut

    • The Kordofan giraffe is a subspecies of the giraffe found in northern Cameroon, southern Chad, the Central African Republic, and possibly western Sudan. The Kordofan giraffe is also known to be the smallest giraffe species only growing up to 3.8 to 4.7 meters.

Monkeys

  • Cookie

    • The golden lion tamarin, also known as the golden marmoset, is an endangered species. The range for the golen lion tamarin spreads across four places along southeastern Brazil. The golden lion tamarin gets its name from its bright reddish orange fur and the extra long hairs around the face and ears which give it a distinctive mane.

  • Earl

    • Common squirrel monkey is the traditional common name for several small squirrel monkey species native to the tropical areas of South America. The common squirrel monkeys usually travels in bands or troops which can number from 12-100. Ocassionally, troops as large as 500 has been witnessed.

  • Banana Pudding

    • The Japanese macaque, also known as snow monkey, is a terrestial monkey species that is native to Japan. The Japanese macaque is the only species of monkey in Japan. The Japanese macaques lives in matrilineal societies, where the females stay in their natal group for life, and the males move out before they are sexually mature.

Alligators

  • Wren

    • The American alligator, sometimes referred to as a gator or common alligator, is a large crocodillian reptile native to the Southeastern United States. Adult male American Alligators can measure up to 11.2 to 15.1 feet in length and weigh up to 1100 pounds. Females are smaller, measuring up to 8.5 to 9.8 feet.

  • Aspen

    • The Chinese Alligator, also known as also known as the Yangtze alligator, China Alligator, or muddy dragon, is only found in China. The Chinese alligator grows to 5 to 7 feet and weighs 80 to 100 pounds as an adult. It brumates and burrows in winter and is nocturnal in the summer.

  • Mika

    • The Black Caiman is a crocodilian reptile, only native to South America. With a maximum length of around 16 to 20 feet and a mass of over 1000 pounds. The black caiman is one of the largest predators in the Amazon basin and the largest member of the Alligatoridae family, making it one of the largest extant reptiles.
